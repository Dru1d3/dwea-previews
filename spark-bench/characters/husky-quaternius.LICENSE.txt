What ships in this GLB
----------------------
- Quadruped rig: 49 joints (Body, Back, Torso/Torso2/Torso3, Neck1..3, Head,
  Ear1..4 L/R, FrontShoulder L/R, FrontUpperLeg L/R, FrontLowerLeg L/R,
  BackShoulder L/R, BackLeg L/R, BackUpperLeg L/R, BackLowerLeg L/R,
  Tail1..6, plus IK pole-target helpers).
- 12 animation clips (the runtime drops the duplicate `AnimalArmature|*`
  prefixed copies that FBX2glTF emits): Idle, Idle_2, Idle_2_HeadLow, Walk,
  Gallop, Gallop_Jump, Jump_ToIdle, Eating, Idle_HitReact_Left,
  Idle_HitReact_Right, Attack, Death.
- For DWEA-32 parity with the soldier rig: idle ← Idle, walk ← Walk,
  run ← Gallop. The character pipeline lowercases clip names at load time
  and renames the locomotion trio to match the existing humanoid contract.

CC0 means no attribution is legally required. We still credit Quaternius in
the registry's `credit` field — it's the right thing to do and helps future
maintainers track provenance.

Why no separate bake step
-------------------------
The poly.pizza GLB ships ready-to-use; no FBX→GLB conversion is needed (it
was already baked by Quaternius via FBX2glTF v0.9.7). The only runtime
fix-ups are: (a) drop the `AnimalArmature|*` clip duplicates, (b) lowercase
clip names + rename Gallop→run for the player rig's locomotion contract.
Both happen in code, not at bake time.
